Project 2

Honors Thesis: AI and Ethics in Filmmaking

A qualitative thesis exploring how AI can support filmmaking while preserving authorship, consent, and creative integrity.

Research

This thesis began with a question many creators are now facing: if AI can accelerate film production, how do we protect human authorship, consent, and creative integrity at the same time?

To explore that tension, I conducted a qualitative study using literature review and visual media case studies, examining both the practical benefits of AI and the ethical risks around labor, authenticity, and misuse.

The research framework focused on the full filmmaking pipeline (pre-production, production, and post-production), with close attention to themes such as transparency, job security, authorship in AI-generated scripts, and deepfake governance.

The key takeaway is that AI can meaningfully support filmmakers through ideation and workflow efficiency, but it should complement rather than replace human storytelling. Recommendations center on consent-first design, transparent disclosure, and equitable regulation.
