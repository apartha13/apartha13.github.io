About Me

Computer scientist and AI builder grounded in human impact

Based in San Diego, currently pursuing my Master's in Computer Science with a specialization in Artificial Intelligence at UC San Diego.

Building systems that amplify human capability, not replace human judgment.

I began my academic journey at Arizona State University, where I studied Computer Science while also pursuing a minor in Business through Barrett, The Honors College. At ASU, I learned to see technology not only as a technical discipline, but as a tool for solving human problems. My coursework gave me a foundation in algorithms, software engineering, data structures, and systems, while my honors experience pushed me to think more deeply about the social impact of the tools we build. Over time, I became increasingly drawn to artificial intelligence because it sits at the intersection of technical rigor, creativity, and real-world decision-making.

My interest in AI grew through projects that showed me how intelligent systems can support people in meaningful ways. For my honors thesis, I explored the ethical implications of AI in the film industry, focusing on creative integrity, job security, and audience perception. This project helped me understand that AI is not just about automation or efficiency; it also raises important questions about trust, authorship, and human expression. Around the same time, I worked on applied technology projects in healthcare and accessibility, including a mixed-reality rehabilitation application for the Meta Quest 3 and an engineering project designed to reduce fall risk during chest X-rays. These experiences showed me that the most powerful technologies are not the ones that replace human judgment, but the ones that extend human capability.

Professionally, my internships at Clinisys and Simac Technik gave me the opportunity to build software in real-world environments where reliability, collaboration, and user impact mattered. At Clinisys, I worked on production software for laboratory systems, improving workflows used by healthcare organizations. At Simac Technik in Prague, I developed automation tools that supported quality assurance and reporting. These experiences taught me how to translate technical ideas into practical systems, and they reinforced my interest in building AI and data-driven tools that people can actually use, trust, and understand.

Today, as I pursue my Master’s in Computer Science with a specialization in Artificial Intelligence at UC San Diego, I am continuing to explore how AI can be designed responsibly and applied effectively. My work spans machine learning, data-centric AI, retrieval-augmented generation, mixed reality, and human-centered software systems. Across these projects, I am motivated by the same question: how can technology help people make better decisions, access better tools, and feel more supported in complex environments?

This portfolio represents my growth as a computer scientist, researcher, and builder. It brings together projects from my academic, professional, and personal experiences that reflect my interest in applied AI, ethical technology, and meaningful problem-solving. Whether I am building a machine learning model, designing an interactive system, or studying the societal impact of AI, my goal is to create technology that is technically strong, thoughtfully designed, and grounded in human needs.
