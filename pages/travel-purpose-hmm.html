Project 4

Decoding Travel Purposes Through Sequential Probabilistic Models

A collaborative mobility research project that followed a simple question: if a traveler’s route and time of day are visible, can we infer what they are likely doing next?

Story

From noisy trips to latent intent

We started with a mobility dataset full of trip modes, timestamps, and purpose labels, and quickly ran into the core challenge: the thing we cared about most was never available at inference time.

That made the project less about memorizing labels and more about reading patterns across a day, where one trip’s mode and timing could hint at the next hidden purpose.

Hidden Markov Models became the natural lens for that story: purposes were the hidden states, and travel modes were the clues.

Model + Architecture

We used sequential models, then made the observations richer

First we trained first-order, 3-gram, and edge-emitting HMMs with maximum likelihood estimation on fully observed training data, then used Viterbi to decode held-out days.

The turning point came when raw trip modes were no longer the only signal. Once we encoded time of day and later hour of day, the observations became much more informative and the models had a clearer path to follow.

We compared those models against home-only, time-of-day, frequency, and random forest baselines so we could see whether the sequential structure was actually doing useful work.

The pipeline began with GPS traces from Geolife, grouped them by trip, sorted them into daily sequences, and filtered out short or low-quality records before training.

From there, we estimated initial, transition, and emission probabilities directly from counts, which kept the HMM training transparent and easy to inspect.

The architecture also included a higher-order 3-gram version and an edge-emitting variant, letting us test whether more expressive temporal assumptions improved decoding.

In the end, the strongest result came from adding time-based context, which gave the models enough signal to track latent purpose more reliably.
